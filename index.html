Key metrics explained

Click-through rate (CTR): % of delivered emails where someone clicked a link. Higher = more engaging content.
Unsubscribe rate: % of recipients who opted out after that send. Lower = healthier list.
Delivery rate: % of sent emails that actually reached inboxes (not bounced). 99%+ is excellent.
Spam rate: % of recipients who marked the email as spam. 0% is perfect.
Correlation (r): Statistical measure from -1 to +1. Close to 0 = no relationship. Close to 1 = strong positive relationship.
Open rate: % of recipients who opened the email. Manually extracted from HubSpot chart — approximate values (±1–2%). Apple Mail Privacy Protection can inflate open rates.

Open rate & click-through rate over time

Open rates are trending strongly upward — from ~36–43% in summer 2025 to ~48–55% from November onwards. Average open rate: 46.8%, with a peak of 69.4% on 30 Jan. This suggests growing subject line appeal and/or audience engagement over time.

Click-through rate is highly volatile (5.1% to 18.0%) but shows no directional trend — unlike open rates. The spikes at 18% (8 Aug) and 17.8% (20 Feb) show that certain content generates nearly 3× more clicks than others. Content is the dominant factor for CTR.

February 2026 had the highest single-dispatch CTR (17.8% on 20 Feb) but also one of the lowest (5.0% on 27 Feb). Average CTR of 9.7% with low unsubscribes (0.17%).

Open rate vs click-through rate — subject line or content problem?

This scatter reveals the diagnosis. High-open + low-click dispatches (top-left quadrant) had good subject lines but content didn't deliver. Low-open + high-click (bottom-right) had weak subject lines but great content — those readers who did open were highly engaged.

30 Jan is a standout: 69.4% open rate but only 8.8% CTR — the most extreme "subject line overpromise" case. The audience opened in huge numbers but didn't find compelling links to click.

1 Aug & 15 Aug were double lows: Open rates of 28.4% and 35.7% combined with CTR of 10.9% and 7.3% respectively. These dispatches struggled on both subject line appeal and content engagement.

Overall summary & recommendations

What's going well

Exceptional deliverability (99.4–99.7%, zero spam). Open rates average 46.8% and are trending strongly upward (from ~37% to ~53%), suggesting growing brand recognition. The average CTR (~10%) is well above typical B2B benchmarks (2–5%). Article views have grown 3.3× in 8 months. The dispatch is a proven traffic driver, generating 2.5× more views on send days.

What needs attention

Click-through rate is driven by content, not timing. Milestone stories and policy changes (17–18% CTR) vastly outperform routine monthly benchmarks (5–7% CTR). The ×3.5 gap means there's huge potential in leading each dispatch with high-news-value content.

High open rates don't always mean high CTR. 30 Jan had 69.4% open rate but only 8.8% CTR — the biggest gap in the dataset. Conversely, 8 Aug had 56.8% open and 18% CTR. The open rate–CTR relationship is weak, meaning subject line optimisation and content quality are independent levers.

January 2026 was a tough month — lower CTR and unsubscribe spikes, despite decent open rates (48–69%). This coincides with the "Automation Draft" naming in HubSpot — possible link to a process or template change?

The Weekly list size has stagnated around 9,000–10,000 for 8 months despite article views growing 3.3×. The growing readership isn't converting to subscribers at scale.

Priority action items

1. Lead with milestone/event stories. Dispatches featuring capacity milestones, market design changes, and multi-market roundups consistently hit 14–18% CTR. Routine monthly benchmarks land at 5–8%. Prioritise "news" over "data updates" in the dispatch headline.

2. Address the open-to-click gap. Several dispatches show high open rates (50%+) but below-average CTR — the classic "subject line overpromise" pattern. When open rates are high, focus on link placement and content value within the email to convert openers into clickers.

3. Investigate the 6 Feb views spike. 1,939 views on a day with only 8.9% CTR suggests a powerful non-email traffic source. Identifying it could unlock a new growth channel.

4. Convert growing readership into subscribers. Article views tripled while the list stayed flat. Adding email capture CTAs on high-traffic articles could bridge this gap.
